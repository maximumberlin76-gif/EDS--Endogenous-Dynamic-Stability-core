# Examples

# 1. Economic System

## Mapping

- S(t) — structural synthesis: the process of formation and maintenance of structural integrity and self-organization through infrastructure development, knowledge accumulation, industrial growth, and human capital formation;

- P(t) — destabilizing structural pressure: destabilizing extraction, debt pressure, fragmentation of productive structure, speculative overload;

- D(t) — irreversible structural losses: corruption, systemic inefficiency, irreversible degradation of infrastructure, institutional decay;

- C(t) — parameter of endogenous structural coherence determining the level of structural integrity and dynamic stability of the economic system over time.

## Regimes

If:

`S(t) > P(t) + D(t)`

→ positive structural formation balance emerges, and formal structural existence remains accessible.

If:

`S(t) ≈ P(t) + D(t)`

→ quasi-stationary operational balance emerges.

If:

`S(t) < P(t) + D(t)`

→ structural degradation and fragmentation propagate.

## Stability Condition

Real dynamic stability requires:

`C(t) > P(t)`

## Insight

GDP growth alone does not guarantee dynamic stability.

Only sufficient endogenous structural coherence preserves structural integrity and dynamic stability of the economic system over time.

- D(t) — irreversible structural losses: computational waste, irreversible redundancy, scaling inefficiency, error amplification;

- C(t) — parameter of endogenous structural coherence determining the level of structural integrity and dynamic stability of the AI system over time.

## Observation

Modern AI systems may:

- increase S(t) in narrow operational domains;
- increase D(t) through scaling inefficiency and computational waste;
- increase P(t) through infrastructure demands and recursive operational complexity.

## Structural Existence Condition

Formal structural existence requires:

`S(t) ≥ P(t) + D(t)`

## Dynamic Stability Condition

Real dynamic stability requires:

`C(t) > P(t)`

Otherwise:

- AI systems may become energy-intensive amplifiers of operational noise, complexity, and structural dissipation.

# 5. Physical System: Laser

## Mapping

- S(t) — structural synthesis: the process of formation and maintenance of an organized operational regime through population inversion formation and retained organized energy structure;

- P(t) — destabilizing perturbations and dissipation pressure;

- D(t) — irreversible optical and energetic losses;

- C(t) — parameter of endogenous structural coherence determining the level of structural integrity and dynamic stability of the coherent emission regime over time.

## Regimes

Below operational threshold:

`S(t) < P(t) + D(t)`

→ stable coherent operational regime cannot emerge.

Above operational threshold:

`S(t) > P(t) + D(t)`

and:

`C(t) > P(t)`

→ stable coherent emission regime becomes operationally accessible.

# Final Insight

Across all examined systems:

`Δ(t) = S(t) − P(t) − D(t)`

describes formal structural existence through the balance of structural synthesis, destabilizing pressure, and irreversible losses.

But positive `Δ(t)` alone does not guarantee real dynamic stability over time.

Real dynamic stability requires:

`C(t) > P(t)`

where `C(t)` is the parameter of endogenous structural coherence determining the level of structural integrity and dynamic stability over time.

# Key Result

The same operational distinction appears across multiple domains:

formal structural existence is not equal to dynamic stability and structural integrity over time.

This suggests a general operational principle for open nonlinear dynamical systems:

a structure may exist through positive formation balance, but it remains dynamically stable over time only while endogenous structural coherence exceeds destabilizing structural pressure.
