# Marnov Criterion of Endogenous Dynamic Stability

# Operational Parameter Definitions

Where:

- `C(t) ≥ 0` — parameter of general endogenous structural coherence determining the level of structural integrity and dynamic stability over time;

- `P(t) ≥ 0` — destabilizing structural pressure, operational load, degradation pressure, and fragmentation pressure at time `t`;

- `ε > 0` — minimum positive surplus of endogenous structural coherence over destabilizing structural pressure required for retained dynamic stability over time;

- `t` — operational time variable.

# Necessary Operational Stability Condition

A system remains within a retained dynamically stable structural regime if:

`C(t) ≥ P(t) + ε`

where:

`ε > 0`

Operational interpretation:

- general endogenous structural coherence sufficiently exceeds destabilizing structural pressure over operational time;
- the system retains structural integrity and dynamic stability over time;
- `ε` defines the stability margin between endogenous structural coherence and destabilizing structural pressure.

# Regime Interpretation

If:

`C(t) > P(t)`

→ retained dynamically stable structural regime.

If:

`C(t) ≈ P(t)`

→ critical operational regime with reduced stabilization accessibility.

If:

`C(t) < P(t)`

→ destabilization propagation, loss of structural integrity, and structural fragmentation regime.

# Regeneration and C(t)

Structural regeneration is a continuous endogenous process of restoring and maintaining structural integrity and coherence over time.

Regeneration supports `C(t)`, but is not identical to `C(t)`.

If endogenous processes become decoherent in phase, amplitude, accumulation rate, connection quality, or functional contribution, the structure loses the capacity to regenerate structural integrity faster than dissipation destroys it.

Therefore:

decoherence of endogenous processes → reduction of regenerative capacity → dissipation exceeds regeneration → decrease of `C(t)` → disruption of dynamic stability over time.

# Extended Operational Form

`dC/dt = αC − βP − γ(Δφ)²`

Where:

- `α` — endogenous coherence amplification coefficient;

- `β` — destabilizing-pressure coupling coefficient;

- `γ` — coherence-misalignment destabilization coefficient;

- `Δφ` — operational mismatch of internal structural processes, including but not limited to phase mismatch.

Operational interpretation:

- endogenous structural coherence may increase through retained coherent self-organization and continuous structural regeneration;

- destabilizing structural pressure suppresses endogenous structural coherence and dynamic stability;

- mismatch of internal structural processes reduces regenerative capacity and increases destabilization propagation;

- if mismatch grows, the structure loses the ability to regenerate structural integrity faster than dissipation destroys it.

# Notes

This condition represents a necessary operational criterion for retained dynamic stability and structural integrity over time.

It is not, by itself, a sufficient condition for complete structural self-organization, full phase coherence, or complete regime stabilization.

The criterion must not be interpreted as a replacement for the full EDS distinction:

`Δ(t) > 0` describes formal structural existence.

`C(t) > P(t)` describes real dynamic stability over time.
